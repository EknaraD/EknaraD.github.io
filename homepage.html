Primary Care

Description:

Even if you aren't feeling unwell, you should schedule frequent health visits with your primary care doctor. Routine health maintenance is critical for preventing and diagnosing major diseases and chronic ailments that may not have obvious signs at first. In other words, your primary care physician should be your initial point of contact for routine check-ups and exams.

Aside from that, you may talk to your primary care physician about non-emergency medical matters including reproductive health, diet, sleeping problems, stress management, mental health concerns, and minor illnesses and accidents. Your primary care physician is intimately familiar with your medical history and the drugs you use. As a result, they are best positioned to provide you with the finest long-term health solution possible.


Urgent Care

Description:

Urgent medical conditions are ones that, while not life-threatening, demand immediate care. So, for those wondering, "Can I use urgent care as primary care if my regular physician is on vacation or has a full schedule?" the answer is yes. However, keep in mind that none of the medical staff you encounter will have as much understanding of your medical history as your PCP, so using urgent care instead of your PCP on a regular basis is not recommended. Patients are typically required to complete a large amount of documentation to assist their regular physician in catching up.

Most patients benefit from urgent care facilities being available 24 hours a day, seven days a week, and not requiring an appointment. So, for a rapid diagnosis and pain management, they provide a handy alternative to waiting for a primary care visit or standing in line for hours at the emergency department. Can you receive a referral from urgent care? Yes, a healthcare expert at an urgent care facility can provide you a specialist referral if necessary.
